Bhagat Singh

Born: September 28, 1907

Place of Birth: Village Banga, Tehshil Jaranwala, District Lyallpur, Punjab (in modern day Pakistan)

Parents: Kishan Singh (father) and Vidyavati Kaur (mother)

Education: D.A.V. High School, Lahore; National College, Lahore

Associations: Naujawan Bharat Sabha, Hindustan Republican Association, Kirti Kisan Party, Kranti Dal.

Political Ideology: Socialism; Nationalism; Anarchism; Communism

Religious Beliefs: Sikhism (childhood and teen); Atheism (youth)

Publications: Why I Am An Atheist: An Autobiographical Discourse, The Jail Notebook And Other Writings, Ideas of a Nation

Death: Executed on March 23, 1931

Memorial: The National Martyrs Memorial, Hussainwala, Punjab

Bhagat Singh is considered to be one of the most influential revolutionaries of Indian Nationalist Movement. He became involved with numerous revolutionary organizations and played an important role in the Indian National movement. He died a martyr at the age of just 23 years. Following his execution, on March 23, 1931, the supporters and followers of Bhagat Singh regarded him as a "Shaheed" (martyr).

Achievements

Bhagat Singh's politically active Sikh family supported the Ghadar Movement, which aimed to overthrow British rule in India. His exposure to violent incidents during his teenage years shaped his strong patriotic outlook. He initially supported the Indian National Congress and Gandhi's non-violent principles but became disillusioned after Gandhi called off the Non-Cooperation Movement. At the age of 16, Bhagat Singh joined the Young Revolutionary Movement and dedicated himself to armed revolution against the British.

HE WAS FAMOUS AS AN IMPECCABLE ORATOR AND WRITER

Bhagat Singh joined National College in Lahore in 1923, an institution established by Lala Lajpat Rai to oppose and boycott the British government. He won an essay competition in 1923 and worked for Pratap, a revolutionary weekly in 1924, where he wrote articles and distributed pamphlets outlining principles of a violent uprising against the Britishers. Singh also participated in the College Dramatics Society, where he gained quick prominence due to his oratory and writing skills. Lahore provided a platform for Singh's political work and struggle for freedom.

HE BECAME A LEADER OF HINDUSTAN REPUBLICAN ASSOCIATION

Bhagat Singh joined the Hindustan Republican Association in 1924, a radical group that aimed to foment revolution against the British Raj through armed revolution. The HRA conducted several robberies and raids to raise funds to acquire arms and ammunition, including the famous Kakori Train Robbery. Bhagat Singh and Chandra Shekhar Azad emerged as the leaders of the HRA after several of its members were hanged or imprisoned for their activities.

HE FOUNDED NAUJAWAN BHARAT IN 1926

Bhagat Singh set up the Naujawan Bharat Sabha in 1926, an organization that aimed to gather worker and peasant youths from different communities to fight against British colonialism and injustice. The association organized protests, lectures and public meetings to promote revolutionary ideas of freedom, equality and economic emancipation. The members were chosen for their commitment to the country's interests above their respective communities.

HE SERVED AS AN ICON FOR REVOLUTIONARY YOUTHS ACROSS INDIA

Bhagat Singh's intense patriotism and idealism made him an iconic figure for the youth of his generation. He founded an organization that instilled revolutionary spirit in students and led to the founding of youth leagues and student unions across India. His influence on the youth made him a person of interest for the British Government, and he was arrested in 1927. During his trial for murder in 1929, he refused to apologize for his actions, believing he had done no wrong, and justice had been served to the British for the assassination of Lala Lajpat Rai.

HE BOOSTED REVOLUTIONARY MORALE WITH THE ASSASSINATION OF J.P. SAUNDERS

In 1928, Lala Lajpat Rai led a procession against the Simon Commission, and the police used a brutal Lathi Charge to disperse the crowd. Rai was severely injured and died on November 17, 1928. On December 17, 1928, Bhagat Singh and his associates shot and killed assistant superintendent of police John Saunders in retaliation. The assassination did not go according to plan, and their primary target, James A. Scott, could not be killed. The three revolutionaries fled the scene in a planned escape, and the success of the Saunders murder brought prestige to the revolutionaries and created much sensation among the students.
